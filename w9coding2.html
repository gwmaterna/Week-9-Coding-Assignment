The Witcher 3 is the best game I have ever played. I spent well over one hundred hours finishing this game and I truly loved every minute of it. The story is wonderfully engaging, as you are searching for your adopted daughter Ciri, who is also a witcher and on the run from the Wild Hunt, who are a group of spectral warriors. Ciri is a Child of the Elder Blood, the last heir to an ancient Elven bloodline that grants her the power to manipulate time and space.



Awesome, right? Well, it is. But what puts the game above and beyond almost all others are the side quests. They are not your typical cut and paste missions that you perform over and over and over again. They are so well written, so creative, and so memorable that it makes the entire playthrough a pleasure. There is always someone new to meet and something new to defeat. There are dozens and dozens of characters, all with an interesting story to tell.



By the end of your playthrough, you will have faced over one hundred different types of enemies. They get more difficult as you progress through the game. You will need to constantly be upgrading your weapons, armor, spells, and potions in order to defeat them.
